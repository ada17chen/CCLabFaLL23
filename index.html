1. Burj Khalifa Dubai

1. Height Records: 828 meters (2,717 feet)

2. Observation Deck: The tower boasts the world's highest observation deck

3. Elevator Speed: Its elevators are among the fastest globally, reaching speeds of up 600 meters per minute.

4. Abu Dhabi and the United Arab Emirates government lent Dubai money to pay its debts (costs 1.5 billion dollars)

2. Shanghai Tower (632 m / 2,073 ft)

1. Twisting Structure: The Shanghai Tower's unique twisting design reduces wind loads by 24%, allowing it to withstand typhoon-force winds common in Shanghai.

2. Sky Gardens: The tower features 9 sky gardens, creating public spaces for workers to enjoy greenery and natural light as they ascend.

3. Double Skin Facade: Its incorporates two layers of glass, enhancing energy efficiency by providing insulation and reducing heat (costs 2.5 billion dollars)

3. Fairmont Makkah Clock Royal Tower Mecca, Saudi Arabia (601 m / 1,972 ft)

1. Clock Face: Its clock face is the largest in the world, measuring over 43 meters (141 feet) in diameter, visible from several miles away.

2. Pilgrimage Destination: Near the Grand Mosque, it serves as a significant destination for pilgrims during Hajj and Umrah. Its location reflects a harmonious integration of modernity with the sacred surroundings, making it a unique and symbolic structure in the heart of the Islamic world.

3. It is the world's second most expensive building, with the total cost of construction totaling 15 Billion dollars.

4. Goldin Finance 117 TianJin 600 m (1,957 ft)

1. Incomplete Construction: Despite being structurally topped out, the Goldin Finance 117 remains unfinished due to financial challenges, giving it the nickname "The Evergrande Tower".

2. It holds the record for being the tallest unoccupied building and Chow Tai Fook developed it.

3. Its has a unique structural design of a diamond-shaped crown (costs 1.2 Billion dollars)
